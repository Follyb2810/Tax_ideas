## **Nigeria’s 2026 Tax Reform — Simple Breakdown**

### **What’s Changing?**

Nigeria completely redesigned its tax system starting **January 1, 2026** to make taxes simpler, fairer, and more digital.
A new agency called the **Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS)** replaces FIRS.

---

## **Key Points You Need to Know**

### **1. Personal Income Tax (PIT)**

- First **₦800,000 per year is tax-free**.
- Most people (about **90–98%**) will **pay less or no tax**.
- High earners pay a bit more.
- Freelancers/remote workers earning from abroad must now register and pay tax.

---

### **2. Corporate Tax (CIT)**

- **Small businesses (<₦50m turnover)** pay **0% tax**.
- Big companies’ tax drops from **30% → 27.5% (2026)**.
- Export profits are tax-free if the money enters Nigeria through official channels.

---

### **3. VAT**

- Essentials like **food, rent, books, medicines, electricity transmission** = **0% VAT**.
- VAT stays at **7.5%**.
- Businesses must use **e-invoicing** from 2026.

---

### **4. Other Notable Changes**

- Capital Gains Tax for companies rises to **30%**.
- No tax on **crypto gains below ₦800k** yearly.
- High transaction accounts (₦5m+ monthly) get auto-reported to tax authorities.

---

## **Why This Matters**

- **More money in people’s pockets**, especially low/middle income earners.
- **Small businesses get big relief**, boosting growth.
- **Tax evasion becomes harder** due to digital tracking (TIN + BVN + NIN).
- Some worry about **enforcement issues** or businesses passing costs to customers.

---

## **What You Should Do**

- Individuals: Get a **TIN** and keep track of your income.
- Businesses: Prepare for **e-invoicing** and confirm tax category.
